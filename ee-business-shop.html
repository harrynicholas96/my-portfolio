EE Business Shop

Project Overview

Client: EE Business Shop
Project Duration: First phase - 6 months, Next phase - 4 months-present
Role: Product Designer
Tools Used: Figma - Prototyping, Mural - Discovery, Planning

Introduction
The EE Business Shop project was a comprehensive redesign and migration effort aimed at enhancing the user experience for EE's business customers. The project involved migrating the existing content management system (CMS) to a custom-built CMS, converting the Loop Design System to BT's new Arc Design System with EE theming, and optimising the overall user experience through usability testing and stakeholder collaboration.

Approximately 65% of EE Business Shop users were mobile users.

Phase 1: Migration and Design System Conversion

Objective: Migrate the existing CMS to a custom CMS and convert the Loop Design System to BTâ€™s new Arc Design System with EE theming.

Challenges:

  • Migrating content without disrupting the user experience.
  • Integrating the new design system to reflect EE's brand while maintaining BT's business-themed DS.
  • UX enhancements were out scope due to the tight deadline, however any time-feasible improvements that could be backed by research were implemented.

Approach:

  • Collaborating with Design System Team to implement token swapping into BT's design system for business, to rebrand in EE theming.
  • Completed an audit of EE's legacy Design System 'Loop' and liased with BT's design system to adapt based on this library. This allowed BT Group to have a strategic business design system used across its brands.

Phase 2: Post-Migration Optimisation

This is an ongoing project, so high fidelity wireframes can't be shown yet.

Objective: Uncover problems and optimise the user experience of the EE Business Shop post‑migration.

Usability Testing:

  • Interviewed users and gave them a demo of the migrated journey. Users highlighted issues with finding it difficult to compare plans and found it challenging to purchase multiple devices in one journey
  • Users were asked open-ended questions as we wanted to discover more about their needs. Usability of the product was also taken into account, with a keen focus on the mobile experience.

Design Workshop:

  • Facilitated a multi-stakeholder design workshop to brainstorm ideas and solutions for enhancing the EE Shop experience.
  • Engaged stakeholders from various departments to ensure a holistic approach to design improvements.
  • Tried to compromise with business-minded stakeholders, to focus on the findings of the customer research. We knew customers not being able to buy multiple devices in the same journey was a huge pain point so the design team pushed hard for this feature.

Ideation and Prototyping:

  • Developed low fidelity ideations based on insights gathered from usability testing and the design workshop.
  • Prepared a prototype ready for user testing, that could be used comparitively against the previous journey

Low/Mid fidelity wireframes in ideation stage, experimenting with how to make plans easier to compare for mobile users, as per the testing feedback.
